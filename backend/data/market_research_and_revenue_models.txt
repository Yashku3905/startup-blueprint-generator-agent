# Startup Market Research, Common Revenue Models, and Budget Allocation Guidelines

## 1. Market Research & Sizing Frameworks
Every startup must quantify its market size to prove scalability to investors.
- **TAM (Total Addressable Market)**: The overall revenue opportunity that is available if 100% market share is achieved (e.g., Global Smart Agriculture market size).
- **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**: The portion of TAM that fits your product's geographic and feature reach (e.g., Smart Agriculture drone market size in India).
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**: The subset of SAM that you can realistically capture within the first 3-5 years (e.g., Smart Agriculture drone market in Western and Southern India).

## 2. Common Startup Revenue Models
How the business generates revenue and captures value.

1. **SaaS (Software-as-a-Service)**:
   - Users pay a recurring fee (monthly/annually) to access software.
   - Example: Zoho, HubSpot.
   - Moat: High recurring revenue (MRR/ARR), high gross margins, and customer lock-in.

2. **Marketplace & Transaction Fee**:
   - Platform connects buyers and sellers, taking a percentage commission of each transaction.
   - Example: Uber, Swiggy, Airbnb.
   - Moat: Network effects (more buyers attract more sellers and vice-versa).

3. **Direct-to-Consumer (D2C) / E-commerce**:
   - Selling physical products directly through a digital storefront, eliminating middle-men.
   - Example: Nykaa, Mamaearth.
   - Moat: Brand loyalty, customer data control, higher gross margin.

4. **Freemium & Subscription**:
   - Basic features are free; advanced features require a subscription.
   - Example: Canva, LinkedIn Premium.

5. **Ad-Supported / Monetization of Data**:
   - Free tool/platform that aggregates user attention and serves targeted advertisements.
   - Example: Facebook, Duolingo (partially).

## 3. Early-Stage Budget Allocation Guidelines
For an initial seed funding of INR 20 Lakhs to 50 Lakhs (e.g. from Startup India Seed Fund), the general standard allocation of capital is:

*   **Product Development & Engineering (40%)**:
    - Core MVP creation, cloud infrastructure (AWS/Azure/IBM Cloud), hiring software developers/designers.
*   **Marketing, Sales & Customer Acquisition (30%)**:
    - Digital ads (Google/Meta), sales collateral, SEO, content marketing, attending conferences.
*   **Operations & Infrastructure (20%)**:
    - Office rent (or co-working spaces), software subscriptions, hardware assets, customer support.
*   **Legal, Compliance & Reserve (10%)**:
    - Incorporation fees, DPIIT registration, patent/trademark filing, retainer for accounting, audit fees, emergency contingency cash.

### Key Financial Metrics to Track:
- **Burn Rate**: The rate at which the company spends its capital (monthly cash outflow).
- **Runway**: The amount of time (in months) the company has before running out of money. Formulated as: `Current Cash Balance / Monthly Burn Rate`. Recommended runway is at least 12 to 18 months.
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**: Total marketing spend / number of new customers acquired.
- **LTV (Lifetime Value)**: Total revenue expected from a single customer over their relationship lifecycle. Healthy startups aim for `LTV > 3 * CAC`.
